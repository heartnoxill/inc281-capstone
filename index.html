What is medical marijuana?

Medical marijuana is used to treat diseases or conditions by using the marijuana plant or its chemicals. It's essentially the same as recreational marijuana, but it's used for medical reasons. Cannabinoids are a group of chemicals found in marijuana plants that number in the hundreds. Each has a distinct effect on the human body. The main chemicals used in medicine are delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) and cannabidiol (CBD). When people smoke marijuana or eat foods containing THC, they experience a "high."
